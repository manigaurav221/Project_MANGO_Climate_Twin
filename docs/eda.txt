6. Project recommendations
For modeling / ML
Treat rainfall as classification + regression — predict wet/dry first, then amount on wet days (69% zeros hurt MSE).
Use monsoon as a season feature — Jun–Sep vs rest explains 73% of rain variance.
Add diurnal range — strongest rain correlate among temp variables.
Aggregate rainfall to 1° before training on merged data — avoids overweighting cells with 16 sub-points per temp cell.
Consider lags — same-day rain–temp links are weak; weekly/monthly aggregates may work better.
For the Climate Twin dashboard
Map layers: Mean rain heatmap (west coast + northeast wet; northwest dry) and max temp (central hot, north cooler).
Time series: Daily national rain sum + mean temp clearly shows monsoon onset (June) and withdrawal (Oct).
Alert thresholds: Heavy (≥50 mm), heat (≥40°C), cold (≤5°C) counts are ready to use.
Data gaps to be aware of
Only 2013 — no inter-annual climate variability in current data
Rainfall at 0.25°, temp at 1° — resolution mismatch handled via lat_match/lon_match
1.4% rainfall rows lack temp match (edge cells)
